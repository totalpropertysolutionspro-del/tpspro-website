VCT Strip, Wax & Buff

Vinyl Composition Tile (VCT) is the most common hard floor surface in commercial properties throughout the Capital Region — found in schools, medical offices, retail stores, student housing, and commercial kitchens. Over time, VCT floors lose their finish and accumulate ground-in dirt that regular mopping can't remove. TPS Pro provides complete VCT strip, wax, and buff service:

  • Full strip of old wax buildup using commercial-grade strippers
  • Machine scrubbing and rinse
  • Application of 3–5 coats of commercial floor finish
  • High-speed buffing to achieve a mirror-grade shine
  • Regular maintenance buffing schedules available

LVT Cleaning & Maintenance

Luxury Vinyl Tile (LVT) is increasingly common in updated commercial spaces, apartments, student housing, and renovated properties throughout Albany, Saratoga County, and the Capital Region. LVT requires different care than VCT — it should not be stripped or heavily waxed, but responds exceptionally well to professional deep cleaning, appropriate pH-neutral solutions, and protective finish application. TPS Pro's crews are trained to treat LVT surfaces correctly, preserving the finish and extending the floor's lifespan.

How Often Should Commercial Floors Be Professionally Serviced?

Commercial floor care frequency depends on foot traffic, floor type, and the standard of appearance your business requires. General guidelines TPS Pro uses for Capital Region commercial properties:

  • VCT floors (strip & wax) — full strip and recoat annually or semi-annually for most commercial properties; high-traffic medical offices and schools may need quarterly. Interim buffing monthly or quarterly keeps the finish bright between full strip jobs.
  • Carpet deep extraction — quarterly for high-traffic commercial carpets (offices, hospitality, healthcare); semi-annually for lower-traffic spaces. Spot treatment should happen immediately when stains occur.
  • LVT maintenance — monthly professional cleaning for high-traffic LVT; quarterly for moderate-traffic spaces. LVT does not need stripping but benefits from a periodic protective finish application (annually).
  • Tile and grout scrubbing — quarterly for restroom tile; semi-annually or annually for lobby and kitchen tile depending on usage.

TPS Pro can assess your specific floors and recommend a maintenance schedule that keeps your property looking sharp without over-servicing. Call (518) 948-7156 to schedule a walk-through and assessment.

VCT vs. LVT: What Your Floor Needs

Property managers and building owners across Albany frequently confuse VCT and LVT — they look similar but require completely different care. Using the wrong products or methods can permanently damage a floor. Here's the key difference:

  • VCT (Vinyl Composition Tile) is a porous, old-school floor tile found in older commercial buildings, schools, medical offices, and most commercial kitchens in Albany. VCT is designed to be stripped and waxed — the floor's finish system is the protection. Without regular strip and recoat cycles, VCT becomes permanently dull and starts to absorb stains. TPS Pro uses commercial-grade strippers and 3–5 coats of commercial floor finish on VCT.
  • LVT (Luxury Vinyl Tile) is the newer, more resilient product found in updated apartments, student housing, retail, and modern office spaces. LVT has a factory-applied wear layer that does NOT need stripping — in fact, stripping LVT can destroy it. LVT requires pH-neutral cleaners, appropriate burnishing, and periodic protective finish — not the wax system used on VCT.

TPS Pro assesses your floor type before every job and applies the correct methods. We'll never strip a floor that shouldn't be stripped.

How long does VCT strip and wax take?
For a typical 2,000–3,000 sq ft commercial floor, a full strip and recoat takes approximately 6–8 hours including dry time between coats. Larger spaces are proportionally longer. We strip the old finish, machine scrub, apply 3–5 coats of commercial finish, and allow proper dry time between each coat. The floor should cure fully within 24 hours before heavy equipment use.
