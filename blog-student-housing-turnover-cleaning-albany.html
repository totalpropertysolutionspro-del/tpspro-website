Student Housing Turnover Cleaning: The Complete Guide for Albany Property Managers

By TPS Pro  ·  July 2026  ·  10 min read

If you manage student housing in Albany, you know the drill. Leases end July 31st. New tenants move in August. That gives you — at best — two weeks to turn dozens of units from post-finals chaos into spotless, rent-ready apartments. Miss the deadline, and you're looking at empty units, angry owners, and a hit to your reputation.

This guide covers everything Albany property managers need to know about student housing turnover cleaning: the timeline, the checklist, the common mistakes, and why having a professional cleaning partner is the difference between a smooth turnover and a disaster.

Albany Student Housing by the Numbers: UAlbany alone enrolls 17,000+ students. Add Saint Rose, Albany Law, Albany Med, Sage Colleges, and HVCC — the Capital Region's student population tops 50,000. Every August, thousands of those students move.

The Student Housing Turnover Timeline (And Why It's Tight)

Most Albany student leases follow the academic calendar: August 1 to July 31. Property managers in the Pine Hills, downtown Albany, and uptown areas near UAlbany face the same problem every year — compressed turnaround with zero margin for error.

Week 1 (Aug 1–7): Inspection and Cleaning

  • Walk through every unit as tenants move out — document damage, abandoned items, and cleaning needs
  • Schedule cleaning crews immediately — don't wait until all units are empty
  • Prioritize units with earliest move-in dates

Week 2 (Aug 8–14): Repairs and Final Prep

  • Complete maintenance work: paint touch-ups, plumbing fixes, appliance checks
  • Final cleaning pass on all units
  • Pre-move-in walkthrough with owners or leasing agents

The hard truth: if your cleaning vendor no-shows or cuts corners during this window, there's no time to recover. That's why Albany property managers who consistently nail turnover season have a reliable cleaning partner — not a rotating cast of whoever answers the phone.

Student Housing Turnover Cleaning Checklist

Here's what a thorough student housing turnover clean should cover. If your current vendor skips any of these, you're leaving money on the table — and problems in the unit.

Kitchen

  • Inside oven, stovetop, range hood, and drip pans
  • Inside refrigerator and freezer (defrosted, shelves removed and cleaned)
  • Inside microwave — walls, ceiling, turntable
  • Cabinet interiors and exteriors, including hardware
  • Countertops and backsplash degreased
  • Sink, faucet, and garbage disposal sanitized
  • Floor scrubbed and corners checked

Bathroom

  • Toilet deep cleaned inside and out, including base and behind tank
  • Tub, shower walls, and glass doors — soap scum and hard water stains removed
  • Grout scrubbed, not just wiped
  • Vanity, mirror, medicine cabinet interior
  • Exhaust fan dust cleaned
  • Floor corners and baseboards

Living Areas and Bedrooms

  • All floors vacuumed and mopped (corners included — no perimeter dust)
  • Baseboards, door frames, and light switches wiped down
  • Window sills, tracks, and blinds cleaned
  • Closet interiors — shelves, rods, and floor
  • Ceiling fans dusted (blades wiped, not just knocked around)
  • Wall scuffs and marks spot-cleaned

The 3 Most Common Turnover Cleaning Mistakes

1. Treating Every Unit the Same

A three-bedroom house on Hudson Avenue with six students who cooked every meal needs a different approach than a single-occupant studio. Cookie-cutter cleaning leaves units looking "cleaned" but not clean. Assess each unit individually — not by a generic checklist.

2. Rushing the Kitchen and Bathroom

These are the rooms tenants (and their parents) inspect most closely. If the oven still has baked-on residue or the shower has mildew in the corners, you'll get a call-back — and possibly a withheld deposit or a bad review. These spaces demand extra time. Build it into your schedule.

3. No Final Walkthrough With the Cleaning Crew

Always schedule a walkthrough before the crew leaves the property. At TPS Pro, our supervisors spot-check every turnover job before we call it done.

How to Build a Turnover Cleaning System That Scales

  1. Pre-season walkthrough. Two weeks before turnover, inspect every unit and log cleaning needs. Share the list with your cleaning partner before move-out day.
  2. Dedicated cleaning schedule. Assign specific cleaning days per building or zone. Don't let cleaning dates float.
  3. Standardized scope of work. Every unit should be cleaned to the same spec. Your cleaning partner should have a documented standard, not a verbal "do a good job."
  4. Post-clean inspection report. Require photo evidence from your cleaning vendor. TPS Pro provides digital documentation so you know exactly what was done.
  5. Clear communication channel. One point of contact. One text or call gets you status updates on all your units.
