What Is Student Turnover Cleaning?

Student turnover cleaning is a deep clean performed between tenants — typically after a full academic year of occupancy. Unlike a standard move-out clean, student housing often requires more intensive work: carpet staining from a year of heavy use, kitchen buildup, bathroom grout that hasn't been scrubbed in months, and walls that need wiping down throughout.

A proper student turnover clean typically includes:

  • Full deep clean of all kitchen surfaces, appliances, and cabinet interiors
  • Complete bathroom sanitization — toilets, tubs, showers, tile, grout, vanities
  • Carpet cleaning and spot treatment (often a separate line item)
  • Hard floor scrubbing and mopping on all non-carpeted surfaces
  • Interior window cleaning, blinds dusted, window tracks cleaned
  • Walls wiped, baseboards cleaned, light switches and door handles sanitized
  • Trash and debris removal from all rooms and closets
  • Final walkthrough checklist per unit for inspection handoff

Factors That Affect the Price

Not every unit costs the same to turn over. Here are the main variables that affect what you'll pay:

Unit Size and Condition

Larger units take more time, and units in poor condition take significantly more. A 2-bedroom where students have been careful costs far less to clean than a 2-bedroom with heavy kitchen grease, stained carpets, and walls that need scrubbing top to bottom. Most cleaners will either charge a flat rate based on condition assessment or add a surcharge for heavy soil.

Carpet Cleaning

Carpet cleaning via hot water extraction is almost always a separate cost. In Albany, expect to pay $50–$100 per unit for carpet cleaning, depending on square footage. If your units are all hard-surface flooring, you'll pay less overall. Some property managers skip carpet cleaning on units with minimal soiling — TPS Pro can advise per unit after inspection.

Volume of Units

Volume is the single biggest lever for reducing per-unit cost. A single-unit cleanup commands a higher per-unit rate because of mobilization time. At 10+ units, you start qualifying for volume pricing. At 30, 50, or 100+ units, the economics shift meaningfully. TPS Pro handles 100–200+ units per peak season and offers structured pricing for large accounts.

Peak Season vs. Off-Season

Timing matters. Albany's academic turnover window — May through August — is the highest-demand period for cleaning crews. During peak season, demand outpaces supply, which keeps prices at the higher end of the range and means crews book up fast. If you have any flexibility (e.g., phased move-in dates), even a 2-week shift can improve your rate and availability.

Add-On Services

Common add-ons that affect total cost include: refrigerator deep clean (often extra if heavily soiled), oven cleaning, window screen removal and washing, light fixture cleaning, patio or balcony cleaning, and trash-out / debris removal beyond normal move-out. These are typically quoted per item.

What to Look for When Hiring a Student Turnover Cleaning Company

Price matters, but reliability matters more. A cleaning company that quotes low and doesn't deliver inspection-ready results costs you in delayed move-ins and lost rent. Here's what to verify before signing on:

  • Insurance and bonding: Get proof of general liability and workers' comp coverage before crews enter your units.
  • References from property managers: Ask for references specifically from landlords and property management companies — not residential clients.
  • Unit-by-unit checklists: Good turnover crews use standardized checklists and can provide documentation per unit for your inspection records.
  • Scheduling capacity: Can they handle your full volume within your window? Ask directly how many units they can turn per day.
  • Communication and coordination: You need a real point of contact — someone who answers the phone and can update you on progress in real time.
